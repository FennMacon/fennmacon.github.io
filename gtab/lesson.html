SCALES

Ok so basically you know how there are 12 notes? umm I guess let's look at a piano keyboard layout



Yeah so you see how there are 12 notes but it's split up into 7 white notes and 5 black notes right? that's your basic scale. which uses 8 notes. It starts and ends on the same note, the easiest way to think about this is with the "do re mi" thing, which secretly taught you scales the whole time.



Scales dont have to be a set number of notes, most guitar/bass scales are actually pentatonic scales, penta is a prefix that means 5, eg a pentagram or the pentagon or whatever, so a pentatonic scale has 5 notes. This is cool because if you know what key a song is in, you can mess around on that pentatonic scale usually and find some cool lil riffs to play



Check that out, that's pretty scary right? turns out, it isn't. That's just the same thing three times! If you look at the notes, there's only E, G, A, C, and D highlighted. Let's look at the top. All the way on the left is the grayed out one. If you google "bass pentatonic scale" you can see variations on this image. The grayed out ones are replicated in the bass tab below it. That's just a fretboard. You can see the dots on it and they've highlighted the 3rd, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 12th frets. Basically, you can see it just repeats EGACD over and over again.
